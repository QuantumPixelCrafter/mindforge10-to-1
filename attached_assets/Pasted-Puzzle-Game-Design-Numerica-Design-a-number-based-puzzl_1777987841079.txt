Puzzle Game Design – “Numerica”
Design a number-based puzzle game called Numerica, inspired by Make 24 but expanded with customizable settings, multiple modes, and social competition. Players begin each round by choosing between three to six digits, then select a time mode of either sixty seconds, two minutes, or three minutes, or opt for an endless “Play ’til dead” mode where a single mistake ends the run. They can also enable additional settings that allow negative numbers and fractions to appear in puzzles, adding depth and variety.
The game offers four distinct modes that can be combined, but must be structured to prevent loopholes. In Classic mode, the objective is to reach a target number, and a small input box appears to let players specify their desired target between 10 and 99, with 24 as the default. Closest Wins challenges players to get as close as possible when an exact solution isn’t achievable. Balance the Equation requires filling in missing parts of an equation using the given digits and operators. Random Numbers ensures that the digits are fully randomized every round, keeping gameplay unpredictable and fresh. Regardless of mode, the puzzle generator must guarantee that each round is calculable, with at least one valid solution or a defined best possible outcome.
Operators available by default are addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division, with advanced options such as exponentiation, modulus, or factorials unlocked as players progress. Multiplayer functionality allows friends to compete head-to-head, receiving identical sets of digits and targets. Scoring rewards exact solutions with full points, closest solutions with partial points, and includes bonuses for speed and efficiency, with tie-breakers determined by the number of operations used. Progression systems introduce harder targets and new operators, while daily challenges and leaderboards encourage replayability and community competition.
Each round flows smoothly: players select digits, time, and modes, the game generates a solvable puzzle, and they attempt to construct the target number or best solution within the rules. Multiplayer rounds mirror this structure, ensuring fairness by synchronizing puzzles across participants. The overall design emphasizes flexibility, challenge, and social engagement, creating a puzzle experience that feels familiar yet distinctly new.
